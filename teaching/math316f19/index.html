Math 316 - Probability (Fall 2019)

TR 13:20-14:35, McGregory 226

  • Instructor: Joe P. Chen

  • Contact: McGregory 214, (first name).(middle initial).(last name)@uconn.edu.

  • Prerequisites: Solid mastery of the entire calculus sequence (MATH 161-162-163), up to and including series, limits, partial differentiation, and multiple integration.

  • Course synopsis: This is a calculus-based probability course. There will be a mix of theory and applications. We start with putting down the axioms of probability, and illustrate them with coin tosses, die throwing, and card shuffling. Independence and conditioning also come into play. However, (very soon) we graduate to serious discussions of random variables (which is neither random nor a variable---it's a function!). We will cover the major discrete and continuous probability distributions, and discuss their interplays. Then we will cover multivariate distributions, covariances & correlations, conditional distributions, sums of independent random variables. If all goes according to plan, we will end on the law of large numbers and the central limit theorem, the two gems of probability theory.
  • Textbook: We will use the open source textbook here, which contains just a little more than what we will cover. There is no need to purchase a textbook.
  • Optional referencess (to be placed on course reserve at the Cooley Science Library): Ross, A First Course in Probability; Anderson, Seppäläinen, and Valkó, Introduction to Probability.
