Current teaching (Spring 2020)

Introduction to Statistics (CORE 143S)

Real Analysis II (MATH 487): Measure Theory, Fourier Analysis, and Brownian Motion

I am also running an independent study course on stochastic processes, focusing on Markov chains, martingales, introductory stochastic calculus (through the lens of the binomial options pricing model), and the derivation of the Black-Scholes model/PDE. It is loosely based on MATH 3170 which I taught at UConn in Spring '14. But this time I put stronger emphasis on the connection between Markov processes (hitting distributions, hitting times, transition probability) and partial differential equations (harmonic function, Laplace-1 function/torsion, heat equation).
